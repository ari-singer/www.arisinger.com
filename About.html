philosophy statement

I believe that engineers all have the choice on whether they work to design solutions to problems that will positively impact humanity. While it is not always clear what indirect (or even direct) impacts a technological advancement will have on society, if enough engineers go about their career with an overarching goal to use their skills to advance the overall wellbeing of the world and the humans that live on it, the world will be in good hands.     

I currently have many technological interests: machine learning, quantitative finance, web 3.0 and decentralized finance, internet of things, and more. Although I don’t know how my career will shape up in the next decade, I hope to pursue and combine interests to add value to current state of technology. This, in turn, would hopefully benefit society in some way.    

I think that rapid climate change is the biggest threat to the livelihood of humanity — and unfortunately the way society has been built up in the past two centuries is not conducive in making the rapid systemic changes needed to fix the climate issues that are getting worse by the minute. While technology advancement could fix Earth's climate one day, it is important to recognize the political, societal, and economic factors that make it overwhelmingly difficult to create this systemic change in such short notice; some scientists already claim we are at a point beyond any chance of repair. I believe that the world’s governments should buy into getting humans to Mars one day, since our runway of time on Earth is only getting shorter. This is something I hope to work on at some point in my career as well.    

I want to make the most out of my career. It is generally known that a great team of people can make a larger impact than one great team member, so I hope to use my skills as a communicator, leader, and technology enthusiast to motivate others to make the most of their careers with me. I look forward for pursuing whatever lies ahead for me.
